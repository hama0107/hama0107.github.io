History of the Louvre

The Louvre Museum is one of the most famous museums in the world. It is located in Paris, France. The building was originally built as a fortress in the 12th century and later became a royal palace.

During the French Revolution the palace was transformed into a public museum. Today the Louvre houses thousands of artworks from different cultures and historical periods and welcomes millions of visitors every year.

Famous Artworks

The Louvre contains many masterpieces including the famous painting Mona Lisa by Leonardo da Vinci. Other popular works include the ancient sculpture Venus de Milo and the impressive statue Winged Victory of Samothrace.
