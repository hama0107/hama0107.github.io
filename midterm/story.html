History of the Louvre

The Louvre Museum in Paris is one of the most famous museums in the world and has a long history that began in 1190 when King Philip II of France built it as a fortress to protect the city. In the 16th century, King Francis I of France transformed the building into a royal palace and started collecting important artworks, including the famous Mona Lisa by Leonardo da Vinci. During the French Revolution, the palace was turned into a public museum in 1793 so people could see the royal art collection. Over time the museum expanded and gained thousands of artworks from different cultures and historical periods. Today the Louvre holds more than 35,000 pieces of art and is also known for its modern glass pyramid entrance designed by architect I. M. Pei, making it one of the most important cultural landmarks in the world.
