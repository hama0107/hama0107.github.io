History of the Louvre

The Louvre began as a fortress in the 12th century built to protect the city of Paris. Later it became a royal palace where French kings lived.

During the French Revolution, the palace was turned into a public museum. Today it is the largest art museum in the world and contains more than 35,000 artworks.
